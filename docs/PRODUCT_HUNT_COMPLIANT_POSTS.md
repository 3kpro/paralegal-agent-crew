### Post #1: General Discussion - "What Actually Makes Content Go Viral?"
**Forum:** p/general
**URL:** https://www.producthunt.com/p/general
**When:** Anytime (NOT promotional)
**Purpose:** Share insights, invite discussion

```
**Title:** What Actually Makes Content Go Viral? I Analyzed 1M+ Posts to Find Out.

**Body:**

After spending 3 hours on a LinkedIn post that got 3 likes, I went down a rabbit hole analyzing what makes content explode.

Spent months digging into viral patterns across platforms. Here's what the data shows:

## Key Findings:

**1. Hook Quality > Topic Choice (3x more important)**
- First 3 seconds decide 70% of engagement
- "How to use AI" → 20/100 viral potential
- "I replaced my marketing team with AI for $0" → 87/100

**2. Multi-Source Validation Matters**
- Topics trending on 1 platform: 40% viral chance
- Topics trending on 3+ platforms: 87% viral chance
- Cross-platform validation = strongest signal

**3. Timing Matters LESS Than You Think**
- Quality beats schedule 9/10 times
- Tuesday 10am ≈ Saturday 2pm if content is good
- Exception: Breaking news requires immediate posting

**4. Platform-Specific Optimization**
- Twitter: 1200 chars max, controversy works
- LinkedIn: 2400 chars optimal, professional + vulnerable
- Reddit: Value-first, no BS, hates self-promotion
- TikTok: First 3 seconds or scroll

**5. Specificity is a Cheat Code**
- Generic: "Marketing tips" → 15/100
- Specific: "How I got 10K LinkedIn followers in 30 days with $0 ad spend" → 92/100

I built a scoring system based on this research for my own content. Been using it for a few months - accuracy is around 87% so far.

**Questions for the community:**
- Do you optimize for virality or just post authentically?
- What's your biggest content creation frustration?
- Have you noticed similar patterns in your niche?

Happy to share more insights from the research if useful!
```

**Why This Works:**
- ✅ Leads with value (insights anyone can use)
- ✅ Invites active participation (asks questions)
- ✅ Not promotional (doesn't pitch XELORA)
- ✅ Adds to community knowledge

## 🚫 What NOT to Do

**Don't post:**
- "I just launched [product], check it out!" (pure promotion)
- Multiple similar posts across forums (looks like spam)
- "Use code PHHUNT50 for 50% off" (selling, not discussing)
- "Built this while driving Uber - upvote me!" (vote manipulation)
- Links to your product in every post (self-promotion)

**Don't:**
- Post the same content in multiple forums same day
- Ask for upvotes or engagement
- Make it about YOU (make it about THEM)
- Lead with the product (lead with VALUE)

---

## 📋 Posting Strategy

### Safe Approach (Recommended):

1. **Wait 2-3 days after launch** (let PH launch settle)
2. **Pick ONE forum** to start (don't spam multiple)
3. **Lead with value** (insights, lessons, questions)
4. **Engage genuinely** (respond to every comment thoughtfully)
5. **Don't link to product** (unless someone directly asks)

### If Someone Asks "What product?":

```
"It's called XELORA - happy to DM you the link if you're interested! Didn't want to spam the forum with promotion."
```

This shows respect for community guidelines and makes people MORE interested.

## ⏱️ When to Post

- **Best times:** Weekdays 9 AM - 12 PM PST (high traffic)
- **Avoid:** Fridays, weekends (low engagement)

## ✅ Approval Checklist

Before posting, ask yourself:

- [ ] Does this add value to the community (beyond promoting my product)?
- [ ] Am I inviting active participation and discussion?
- [ ] Is this helpful to other makers/founders/creators?
- [ ] Would I upvote this post if someone else wrote it?
- [ ] Am I being genuine and humble (not salesy)?

If all answers are "Yes", post it. If even one is "No", revise.
